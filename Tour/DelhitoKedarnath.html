Tour Description

Here is a detailed 5-day, 4-night itinerary for your Delhi to for ₹/-, covering the sacred pilgrimage destinations of Kedarnath and Badrinath:

- ₹/-

Day 1: Delhi to Haridwar

Morning:
Departure from Delhi: Start your journey early around 6:00 AM for Haridwar (approximately 5-6 hours drive). Enjoy breakfast along the way at a comfortable rest stop.

Afternoon:
Arrival in Haridwar: Check-in at your hotel in Haridwar and freshen up.

Evening:
Visit Har Ki Pauri: Visit Har Ki Pauri, the famous ghats of Haridwar. Attend the Ganga Aarti in the evening, a spiritual experience where the Ganges is worshipped with beautiful rituals.

Night:
Overnight stay in Haridwar.

Day 2: Haridwar to Kedarnath

Morning:
Breakfast & Departure: After an early breakfast, leave Haridwar for Kedarnath (approximately 9-10 hours drive to Gaurikund, the base of Kedarnath). Enjoy scenic views of the Garhwal Himalayas during the journey.

Afternoon:
Arrival at Gaurikund: Reach Gaurikund in the afternoon, and check in at a local guest house. Prepare for the trek to Kedarnath (about 6-7 hours on foot, or you can opt for a helicopter ride to Kedarnath if preferred).

Evening:
Kedarnath Darshan: Visit the Kedarnath Temple, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. The temple is located in a picturesque and serene environment.

Night:
Overnight stay in Kedarnath (at one of the basic guest houses or camps).

Day 3: Kedarnath to Badrinath

Morning:
Kedarnath Darshan: After breakfast, revisit the Kedarnath Temple if you wish to perform additional rituals or simply enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
Return Journey: Start your return trek to Gaurikund (or return via helicopter). From Gaurikund, take the drive to Badrinath (approximately 7-8 hours drive).

Evening:
Arrival in Badrinath: Check in to your hotel in Badrinath.

Night:
Overnight stay in Badrinath.

Day 4: Badrinath Darshan

Morning:
Badrinath Temple Visit: After breakfast, visit the Badrinath Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu. You can also take a dip in the Tapt Kund, a hot spring believed to have healing properties, before entering the temple for darshan.

Afternoon:
Explore Badrinath: Explore the surroundings of Badrinath, including the Adi Shankaracharya Samadhi and the Mana Village, the last village on the Indian side before the Tibet border.

Evening:
Leisure Time: Spend the evening at leisure, absorbing the spiritual atmosphere of Badrinath.

Night:
Overnight stay in Badrinath.

Day 5: Badrinath to Delhi

Morning:
Breakfast & Departure: After breakfast, check out from the hotel and begin your return journey to Delhi (approximately 10-12 hours drive).

Afternoon:
Stop for lunch on the way and enjoy scenic views along the route.

Evening:
Arrival in Delhi: Reach Delhi in the evening, marking the end of your sacred pilgrimage.

Summary of the 5-Day Itinerary:

  • Day 1: Depart from Delhi to Haridwar; visit Har Ki Pauri for the Ganga Aarti.
  • Day 2: Drive from Haridwar to Gaurikund; trek to Kedarnath; Kedarnath Temple visit.
  • Day 3: Return trek to Gaurikund; drive to Badrinath.
  • Day 4: Visit Badrinath Temple and explore the surroundings.
  • Day 5: Drive back to Delhi from Badrinath.

Tour Inclusions:
Accommodation: 4 nights in comfortable hotels (on a twin-sharing basis).
Meals: Daily breakfast and dinner at the hotels.
Transportation: Comfortable vehicle for the entire trip (by road).
Guides: Professional driver/guide for sightseeing.
Sightseeing: All temple visits and scenic spots as per the itinerary.
Price: ₹24,999/- per person (on a twin-sharing basis)

Travel Tips:
Clothing: Pack warm clothes, especially for Kedarnath and Badrinath, as temperatures can drop in the evening, even during summer.
Health: Carry necessary medicines, including those for motion sickness, as the roads can be winding.
Altitude Sickness: Be mindful of the high altitudes, especially in Kedarnath and Badrinath.
Booking Helicopter: Ensure your helicopter tickets (for Kedarnath) are booked in advance, as they can sell out during peak seasons.

This 5-day itinerary provides a spiritually enriching experience at two of the most revered temples in India — Kedarnath and Badrinath, while also offering a scenic and cultural journey through Uttarakhand.
